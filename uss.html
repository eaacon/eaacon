Under Strange Suns

Key Art

This was my submission to the Atomhawk Art Competition!

The theme was Under Strange Suns and I was incredibly excited to tackle a sci-fi concept. Its a bit out of my comfort zone but I'm happy with what I came up with, especially with the time I had.

Two ideas came to mind at the start. Either a "strange" alien planet, with crazy life forms and Moebius-esque landscapes, or "strange" as in not as it should be, with the sun being replaced by an alien carrier ship, Death Star style. I really wanted to try my hand at modelling some ships, And I thought I could play around with some atmosphere, so I went with the latter.

I also used this as an opportunity to practice the workflow of bringing in 3D renders and photobashing.

Software used were primarily Blender and Photoshop.

This is what I was most excited about. As I had around a week, I decided to spend a full day ideating the main spaceship design. I wanted to lean towards "astronaut" rather than futuristic, so I referenced fighter jets and modern space rockets rather than abstract or organic aircraft. Since we're seeing the backside only, I decided a single booster would be the best way to go.

I initially had actual landing gear, but after seeing Destiny's ships float down, I decided this simpler approach would be cleaner.

I wanted to differentiate from the our protagonist's ship with sharp angular lines and spikes, as well as use sun/lens flare motifs to emphasize the role the ship carrier was taking. I kept the models simple, but made sure the silhouette and lights were clearly placed.

After several attempts moving around the ship, carrier, and adjusting the ground, I settled with this composition. It's fairly standard but provides a great view of both the ship and the star carrier as a sun proxy. I decided to still include an actual sun behind, so that the comparison between the real and the alien was clear. It also led to a purple-orange color scheme which I felt fit the tone I wanted.

I then progressively tweaked and added more assets, most were made by me but some foliage were supplemented with megascans.

This was the final render before my paint over, It set up the atmospheric perspective well, and gave me a base I was happy to paint over. My PC doesn't have a graphics card, so although the iteration was quite slow, I was satisfied with the result.

With two days left, I did the final paint over. The main considerations were color, shapes, and texture. Photobashing helped accelerate the various materials, and I cleaned up/fully replaced many of the silhouettes. I redid the entire sky, and did a detail pass over everything.
